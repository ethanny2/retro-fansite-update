Playboi Carti

Jordan Carter, or as he's better known Playboi Carti, is an up and coming Atlanta born rapper that is at the forefront of this generation's Soundcloud rap movement. Born September 13,1996 in Atlanta Georgia, Jordan Carter attended North Springs high school where he was a member of the school's basketball team. Due to an argument with the basketball team's coach Jordan left the team and began focusing on rapping. Soon after Jordan created his first rap on Garage Band called Cry under the name 'Sir Cartier'. The song was met with praise from Jordan's peers, and Jordan continued to release a stream of songs in high school while working retail jobs at a variety of places.

Soon after graduating from North Springs in 2014 and garnering thousands of plays on his Soundcloud tracks, Jordan was signed to Awful Records under the new name Playboi Carti. Awful Records was a group that included other big names in the underground rap community such as Father and Ethereal. On April 2, 2015 Carti in collaboration with Awful Record producers made their first breakout hit "Brokeboi". The song was an immediate hit on both Soundcloud and Atlanta , but it was not until Carti met with fashion designer and social media personality Ian Connor that he really started to take off.

Ian and Carti claim to have known each other through their shared sense of fashion, it was by chance one day that Ian heard a song of Carti's and decidedly pronounced him as the next big thing. With the help of Ian's connections to the A$AP Mob, Carti eventually left Awful Records and signed to A$AP Rocky. Together with A$AP Mob Carti has allegedly collaborated with a series of big names such as Frank Ocean and Skepta. Carti was featured on the recent A$AP Mob mixtape Cozy Tapes mixtape and later signed a deal with Interscope Records.

Playboi Carti's debut mixtape Ca$h Carti has been in production for reportedly nearly 2 years, as Carti and his friends tease fans with snippets from unreleased songs that feature the likes of Lil Uzi, Migo's Quavo and more. Despite Carti's nearly non-existent social media presence, fans continue to anticipate the release of his debut mixtape while frequently checking their Soundcloud feeds for any new Carti songs.
